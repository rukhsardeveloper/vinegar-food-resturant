Signature Saffron Biryani Royale

Our most beloved dish – fragrant basmati rice layered with slow-cooked lamb, infused with precious saffron threads and 23 hand-ground spices. A recipe passed through generations of master chefs.

⏱️90 min
👨‍🍳Chef Raj Kumar
🌶️Medium spice
🍽️Serves 6
What You Need

Ingredients

Serves 6 people | Prep: 30 min | Cook: 60 min

🍚 Rice Base

  • Aged Basmati Rice3 cups
  • Saffron threads1 tsp (soaked)
  • Rose water2 tbsp

🥩 Lamb Masala

  • Lamb shoulder1.5 kg
  • Greek yogurt1 cup
  • Caramelized onions3 large
  • Biryani spice blend3 tbsp

🌿 Aromatics

  • Fresh mint leaves½ cup
  • Ghee (clarified butter)4 tbsp
Method

Step-by-Step Instructions

1
Marinate the Lamb
Combine lamb with yogurt, half the spice blend, ginger-garlic paste. Marinate for at least 4 hours or overnight in the refrigerator.
2
Parboil the Rice (70%)
Wash and soak rice for 30 minutes. Boil in salted water with whole spices until 70% cooked. Drain immediately.
3
Cook the Lamb Masala
In a heavy-bottomed pot, heat ghee. Add caramelized onions, then marinated lamb. Cook on high heat to seal, then simmer for 20 minutes with remaining spices.
4
Layer the Biryani
Layer the parboiled rice over the lamb masala. Drizzle saffron milk, rose water, and remaining ghee. Top with fried onions and fresh mint.
5
Dum (Slow Steam)
Seal the pot with dough or foil. Cook on very low flame for 25–30 minutes. The trapped steam will complete the cooking to perfection.
6
Serve & Garnish
Gently open the pot, mix lightly from the sides only. Garnish with crispy fried onions, mint, and rose petals. Serve with raita and papadum.
👨‍🍳

Chef Raj Kumar's Secret Tip

"The true secret to a royal biryani is patience — never rush the dum process. Use aged basmati for the most aromatic result, and always soak the saffron in warm milk for at least 20 minutes before using. The golden hue will be glorious."
